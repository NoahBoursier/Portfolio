2016 - 9th grade

Inspired by something my coding class teacher said about hiding information in images using RGB values, I decided to make a code in python that hides messages in images. DoseMaster

December 2015 - June 2016 - Biology / Computer Science

A requirement for Biology with Research, a class I took in my Freshman year, was to enter into the Alameda County Science Fair. My friends Uday, Gustavo and I wanted to do a project related to engineering, and a problem we thought of was overdosage on medication. So we developed DoseMaster, a pill dispenser, reminder, and organizer for the forgetful. We made a working prototype using an Arduino Mega microcontroller. The price of DoseMaster is only about sixty dollars for the materials and could probably sell at a reasonable price of 80 to 100 dollars, as compared to other household pill systems on the market which range from 400 to 800 dollars. To build DoseMaster I had to rely on my understanding of physics, digital electronics, mechanical engineering and coding. DoseMaster won first place for mechanical engineering in the Alameda County Science Fair and we then went on to present it in three other technology showcases where it recieved very positive feedback from onlookers.

Twisted Marble - Flameworking

In the summer following eighth grade I again went to Oakland on BART to attend a flameworking class at The Crucible. Flameworking is when you get a glass rod and then you melt it with a concentrated flame and using gravity and tweasers to shape it to your liking. Though I'm writing this long after I've made it, I believe the process included squishing a panel of orange glass in between two rods that had been melted on the ends to get the orange in the center, and following that you need to twist the two halves to produce the swirl. I highly suggest flameworking as it isn't that scary and for me it was relaxing in a way.
